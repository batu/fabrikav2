Blind UI-quality differential · marble_run

v1 shipped Android vs v2 web port: why the menu & UI still feel off

A surface-by-surface experiential comparison — hierarchy, proportion, dimensionality, palette, and juice — against Batu-confirmed Android reference captures. Findings are ranked by severity, then confidence. Lower-confidence items are included with labels, not omitted.

reference: refs/captures/android-basegamelab candidate: main @ 390×844 + 430×932 surfaces: menu · settings · level · pause · result date: 2026-07-06

Bottom line

v2 is not failing on placement — the menu has the banner, board, rail and green CTA roughly where v1 puts them. It fails on three structural things a fidelity pass didn't reach: (1) the menu renders the live gameplay board as a backdrop with the level rail dropped on top of it, so the numbered nodes collide with the marbles; (2) every overlay (settings, pause, result) inherits a cream card + orange primary palette where v1 is blue card + green primary, orange only on the ribbon; and (3) the overlays are opaque page replacements with no ribbon header, no corner-X, and no dimmed scrim — they read like web dialogs, not molded candy modals. Everything else (flat gloss, emoji coins/gears, washed HINT tile) compounds a "cheap" read on top.

Findings · ranked by severity then confidence

F1

Saga rail is drawn on top of the live 3D board — nodes collide with the playfield

P1confidence: high menu

What to look at

In v2, node 4 sits dead-center over the marbles, nodes 5 and 3 punch through the board edges, and the connector line runs straight down through the playfield. In v1 the board is a small discrete sprite at the top and the numbered chain hangs cleanly below it through empty purple space. This clutter is the single loudest "something's wrong" signal on the menu.

Root cause

App.renderMenu() → controller.showMenuScene() renders the gameplay 3D board full-bleed on the shared #scene canvas as the menu backdrop; the DOM saga rail (HomeMenu → SagaMap) is layered on top.

Fix direction · layout/composition

Stop using the gameplay board as the menu backdrop. Render a small static board sprite as the rail's top anchor and route all numbered nodes strictly below it (mirror v1). Change lives in App.mountMenu + HomeMenu vertical order.

F3

Overlay palette inverted: cream card + orange primary where v1 uses blue card + green primary

P1confidence: high settings · pause · result

What to look at

v2 settings, pause and result all render cream cards with orange primary buttons and orange toggles. v1 settings is a blue card with a green CLOSE and green toggles — orange appears only on the ribbon header. The affirmative/primary color has flipped green→orange and the card base blue→cream across every overlay. The menu's own LEVEL button is still green, so v2 contradicts itself surface-to-surface.

Root cause

design/tokens.css: --fab-color-surface:#fff3d7 (cream) and --fab-color-accent:#ff8a3d (orange). Shared UI components resolve primary/affirmative + toggle-on through --fab-color-accent.

Fix direction · token

Point --fab-color-accent at the candy green used by the menu CTA; add a separate --fab-color-ribbon orange token for headers only; set the overlay card surface to the candy blue. Toggle-on follows accent automatically.

F4

Settings lost the orange ribbon banner, corner X-close, blue card, and RESET PROGRESS visibility

P1confidence: high settings

What to look at

v1 has a 3D orange ribbon header ("SETTINGS", gloss + folds), a cyan X close top-right, a blue card, green toggles, a green CLOSE and an underlined RESET PROGRESS link. v2 has a flat cream card, plain flat-blue text "Settings" (no ribbon), no X affordance, orange toggles, and no visible RESET link inside the card.

Root cause

SettingsPage composes mountPageShell (a slide-up page with a plain title slot), not a modal with a ribbon + corner-X. No ribbon-header / corner-X capability exists in packages/ui. RESET is wired in App.resetProgress but did not render in the captured card.

Fix direction · component

Add a ribbon-header slot and a corner X-close to ModalShell/SettingsPage — this is a missing component capability, not just a token. Verify RESET PROGRESS lands inside the visible card.

F5

Overlays are opaque and full-screen — no dimmed scrim, the game/menu behind is gone

P2confidence: high settings · pause · result

What to look at

v1 settings floats over a dimmed menu — the sun medallion is visible through the scrim, preserving depth and context. v2 settings and pause show flat purple around the card; the paused board / menu is not visible behind it. The overlays feel like full page swaps, which kills the "modal sitting above the game" depth cue.

Root cause

Settings/pause/result are PAGE-style opaque surfaces (mountPageShell family). --fab-color-overlay-scrim (rgba(0,0,0,.4)) exists but these surfaces don't sit over the live scene.

Fix direction · layout

Render pause + settings as modals over the live scene with the translucent scrim, not opaque page replacements.

F2

Menu board is oversized, over-tilted, and dominates the composition

P2confidence: medium menu

What to look at

v2's board is large, tilted ~18°, and vertically centered — it is the loudest element and fights the rail. v1's board is small, gently tilted ~8°, and clearly secondary to the chain + CTA. At 430w the collision (F1) is even more pronounced.

Root cause

Menu reuses the full gameplay board scene (scale + yaw) instead of a reduced decorative sprite.

Fix direction · layout

Shrink the menu board, reduce camera yaw toward v1's gentle tilt, push it up as the rail's top anchor. Pairs with F1.

F8

Coin iconography is inconsistent across surfaces and none match v1's gold coin

P2confidence: medium menu · level · result

What to look at

The menu coin pill uses icon-marble-coin.png (a muddy marble/globe that reads as a grey blob at "0"); the in-level HUD coin and HINT cost use a Unicode 🪙 emoji glyph; the result reward "25" has no coin icon at all. Three representations, none as crisp/legible as v1's single gold dollar coin.

Root cause

design/assets/icon-marble-coin.png (menu) vs an emoji glyph in GameController.ts (HUD + hint) vs a bare number in the result reward.

Fix direction · asset

Ship one gold-coin sprite and use it consistently in the menu pill, HUD coin, HINT cost, and result reward; replace the emoji glyphs with the <img>.

F9

Buttons and panels are flat — thin bevels and weak gloss versus v1's chunky candy dimensionality

P2confidence: medium menu · all

What to look at

v2's LEVEL button is a flat green fill with a thin bottom edge and a small corner gloss blob. v1's has a broad top gloss sheen sweeping across, a thick rounded bevel, an inner shadow, and a chunky dark outline. Same flattening on the coin pill and gear. This is a big part of the "cheap" read even when color and position are right.

Root cause

Shared .fab-btn uses --fab-shadow-button (a soft 0 3px 8px web drop-shadow) instead of layered inset-highlight + hard bottom-bevel.

Fix direction · token + component

Richer button treatment: inset top highlight, hard bottom bevel via stacked box-shadows, thicker border. Apply to coin pill + gear too.

F11

In-level HINT tile lost its warm tan/wood fill — reads greyish and disabled

P2confidence: medium level

What to look at

v2's HINT tile is a washed grey-lavender rounded square that reads muted/disabled even when the player can afford it. v1's is a warm tan wood square with purple "HINT" text and a gold coin — clearly an active, inviting affordance.

Root cause

The hint tile uses background: var(--fab-color-surface, #fff3d7) with muted borders; against the purple field it resolves cool/grey rather than warm wood.

Fix direction · style/token

Give the hint tile a dedicated warm tan wood fill token and stronger contrast; make affordable vs disabled visually distinct.

F6

Pause is a wide landscape cream card with flat text links — reads like a debug dialog

P2confidence: medium pause

What to look at

"Paused" + an orange Resume pill + tiny brown "Settings"/"Quit" text links on a wider-than-tall card floating mid-screen, no icons, no scrim. It reads like a debug dialog rather than a casual-game pause modal.

Root cause

Minimal PauseOverlay composition; secondary actions are plain text links, primary inherits the orange accent (F3), backdrop opaque (F5).

Fix direction · component/layout

Vertical card, stacked chunky buttons with iconography, scrim behind (F5), green primary (F3).

F7

Result card breaks v1's reward language: cream+orange, no ribbon, no coin icon, muddy medallion

P2confidence: low result

What to look at

"Level Complete" as plain left-aligned brown text, cream card, a big orange Next button, a reward "25" with no coin icon, a "Replay" text link, a muddy AI-looking medallion, and a stray green hairline at the card's top edge. Confidence is low only because there is no paired v1 result capture — but it clearly departs from v1's established reward language.

Root cause

ResultCard shares the cream surface + orange accent tokens (F3) and the ribbon-less header pattern (F4).

Fix direction · token + component

Apply the F3/F4 fixes; add a coin glyph to the reward number; consider a confetti burst on reveal. Capture a v1 result reference to lock the exact target.

F10

Gear glyph is a flat Unicode emoji, not v1's molded 3D gear

P3confidence: medium menu · level

What to look at

Both the menu gear and the in-level HUD gear render as a flat/outline system U+2699 glyph (see the gear in v2-menu.png and v2-level.png). v1's gear is a solid, shaded, dimensional icon that matches the candy chrome.

Root cause

Menu gear (mr-gear-glyph textContent) and HUD gear (mr-gear-btn) both use the Unicode gear character.

Fix direction · asset

Replace the Unicode gear with a rendered gear sprite matching the chrome.

F12

Background motif is flat pale dots versus v1's embossed marble texture

P3confidence: low menu · level

What to look at

v1 backgrounds carry tonal, embossed marble/bubble spheres; v2 (both menu and level) shows pale flat circles. Minor character loss on an otherwise faithful purple ground — contributes subtly to the "flatter" overall read.

Fix direction · art

Richer, shaded sphere background texture.

F13

Ambient motion / press-state / transition juice unverified — likely thin

P3confidence: low all

What to look at

Static captures only. Menu/level confetti flecks are present (good), but there is no evidence of board ambient motion (v1 ships level-mid.png as an ambient-anim reference), button press-scale states, or screen-to-screen transitions. Flagged rather than asserted.

Fix direction · motion

Verify/add subtle board ambient motion, button press-scale feedback, and screen transitions; run a video capture to confirm.
